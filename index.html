What is SIV?

SIV stands for Special Immigrant Visa. It is a type of US visa dedicated for those nationals of Afghanistan and Iraq who worked with the US military and other projects of the US government in Iraq and Afghanistan. The SIV program for Iraqis started in 2008 for Afghans in 2009. Since then, thousands of Iraqis and Afghans with their families have migrated to the US via this program. It is worth mentioning that the program has very strict eligibility criteria and a very lengthy process. SIV applicants must prove that their lives are in danger becuase of their employment with or on behalf of the US government and they must go through several security screenings and paperworks. The application process normally takes from 2 to several years. Some of the applicants that have been in contact with us through our Facebook page, have said that they have been waiting for more than 5 years but on average, the application process takes about 2.5 years

SIV Reports:

According to the Refugee Processing Center (RPC), from October 2016 to July 2017, a total of 13,540 Afghans (which made 3,847 cases) arrived in the US under the Afghan SIV program; also, a total of 2,004 Iraqis (573 cases) arrived in the US under the Iraqi SIV program during the same period of time.
